The Entrepreneur

Starting and running your own business requires management and entrapreneurship skills. When you develo a product, for instance a new Cultural-tourism route of a local township, you are developing something new and are acting as an Entrepreneur. However, when you start taking visitors on the route you begin to be more of a manager than an Entrepreneur because you are then co-ordinating and controlling business operations.

An Entrepreneur is a person who creates a new business in the face of risk and uncertainty for the purpose of achieving profit and growth by identifying opportunities and assembling the necessary resources to capitalise on those opportunities.

The Intrapreneur

An Intrapreneur is an Entrepreneur who finds business opportunities for someone else. An Intrapreneur works for a boss and does not own the business. Intrapreneurship involves finding an opportunity within and making a profit for the business from this opportunity. Intrapreneur differ from Entrepreneur because they prefer the security of working for an existing business and getting a regular salary rather than having all the risk fo running their own business.

The role of entrepreneurs and intrapreneurs

An entrepreneur is someone who:

  • start his/her own business;
  • manages his/her own business;
  • finds new products/services or opportunities;
  • can think of new ideas and new ways to do things
  • organises and controls resources to make sure the business makes profit;
  • has knowledge and skills to sell or produce a service or product;
  • has enough money or can get money to run the business;
  • is willing to take reasonable and carefully considered risks.

An intrapreneur is someone who:

  • manages an existing business;
  • is chosen by the business owner to manage the business;
  • finds new products/services or opportunities for existing business;
  • can think of new ideas and new ways to do things;
  • organises and controls resources to make a profit for existing business;
  • chooses to have the security of earning a regular salary.

Why people start their own businesses?

  • to be their own boss
  • to do the work they like
  • to increase their existing income
  • to breake away from a boring job
  • if they become retrenched or unemployed
  • if they identify a good business opportunity

Personal qualities of the entrepreneur

  1. Confidence

    2. Entrepreneur are likely to be optimistic people, and usually have good reason to be optimistic. They look at life with a positive attitute, and when they have problems or failures, they do not blame others, but analyse the situation to find the cause of the problem or failure. Instead of blaming theselves, they take action to correct the situation so that their business can survive and grow.

  2. Moderate risk

    3. Entrepreneurs do not take foolish risks. They examine a business opportunity intelligently and weigh up the risk involved before reaching a decision. If after thinking through a situation, they believe the opportunity is reasonable and can be achieved, they do market reasearch and plan towards their business goal.

  3. Responsible

    4. Entrepreneurs prefer to be in control of their own resources and use them to achive the business objectives that they decide on. They get job satisfaction by using these resources to help them to achieve their business plans.

  4. Energy

    5. Entrepreneurs have more energy than the avarage person does. As long hours and hard work are usually needed when starting a business, having lo of energy is important to achive business success.

  5. Identify opportunities

    6. Entrepreneurs are good at finding business opportunities. They are not so concerned about on what can be done tomorrow.

  6. Organising

    7. Entrepreneurs know how to put the right people together to do a task. Effetivrly combining people and jobs allows them to achieve their business ideas and objectives.

  7. Achievement

    8. People think that entrepreneurs start their own business just to make money. This is not true; their main reason for starting a business is to develop a feeling of achievement and success. Making money is just a result of that success.

  8. Immediate feedback

    9. 'Let me knw now how I am doing' is the way the entrepreneur thinks. They want to know if they are succeeding or failing and looking for signs to show if they are achieving their business objectives.

Personal resources of successful entrepreneurs

  1. Knowledge and skills

    2. If you already have the necessary technical skills, you also need to develop management skills to become a successful entrepreneur. Financial, marketing, personnel and general management knowledge is important to have, as is the ability to work with people like your staff and customers.

  2. Contacts

    3. Successful entrepreneurs make sure they meet people who can help their business grow. These people themselves are either good business contacts or can introduce you to other people who are valuable business contacts.

  3. Funds

    4. You need enough money to start a business. You can posible get the money by asking for a loan from a government organisation that gives loans or a bank or building society or some other financial institution.

Economic importance of the entrepreneur

Entrepreneurs play an important part in creating work for unemployed people. With huge unemployment in developing countries, entrepreneurs are the people who give these countries hope of a better economic future. In countries all over the world, small businesses, especially in the informal sector, create more jobs than big businesses.

The merit of entrapreneurship

  1. To gain control over your own destiny

    2. Entrepreneurs are people who want to make decision about what is important to them. They like to take control of their future and want to take responsibility about how to achive their personal and business objectives.

  2. To reach your full potential

    3. Entrepreneurship provides a way for you to do things you are good at and like doing. It gives you an opportunity to do your very best and show yourself and others what you are capable of achieving.

  3. To reap unlimited profits

    4. Although making lots of money is not the most importnt thing for entrepreneurs, their ability to keep the money their business earn is certainly an important factor. Earning good profits is reward for entrepreneurs who work hard and use their knowledge and skills to produce valuable goods and services.

  4. To be recognised for your efforts

    5. entrepreneurs enjoy the loyalty of customers who they have served faithfull over the years. Business deals based on trust and respect are the sign of excellent services and quality products of many established businesses.

Why entrepreneurs fail

  1. Lack of management skills

    2. The main reasons for failure in most of small businesses are management inexperience or poor decision-making ability. In these cases, the manager does not have the leadership ability and knowledge necessary to make the business work.

  2. Lack of experience

    3. Entrepreneurs need to have experience in the type of business they want to run. For example, if you want to start a retail clothing business, you should first work in retail clothing store to get some experience of how the business operates.

  3. Poor control

    4. Good management is important to achieve success. Effective managers know that any successful business needs proper management control.

    • Shortage of capital

      • Many business owners make the mistake of starting their business with too little money. Entrepeneurs are often very positive and optimistic people who believe that their business will be successful. The result is that they often make mistakes about what is needed to make the business successful.

    • Customer credit

      • There is lot of pressure on a small business to sell on credit rather than cash on delivery. You may feel that you can outdo your competitors if you sell on credit, or you may feel you need to sell on credit because your competition does so. Whatever the case the small business owner must control credit sales very carefully.

    • Over investing in fixed assets

      • Entrepreneurs may invest a lot of money in resources like land, buildings, equipment and other fixed assets. This money normally comes from capital needed to run the business. If you over invest in fixed assets when you really need to develop or expand, there is no money available to do so.

    • Poor planning

      • Many entrepreneurs do not realise how important it is to do proper planning. It can have a very harmful effct on their business if they do not plan their company's future right from the start. Creating a strategic plan of action helps the entrepreneur find the best way to complete in business.

    • Stock shortages

      • Customers becomes unhappy when a business does not have enough stock to sell. When this is so, customers can became dissatisfied and not come back to your business.

    • Attitudes

      • Entrepreneurs must be prepered to work hard and to give up many things to make their business succeed. If you thing small business management is easy, you should not start your business. Success requires hard work and lot's of it!

    • Ethics

      • Managers must practise ethical behaviour. This means they must be honest in the way they run their business. entrepreneurs who cheat or are dishonest may score for a short period, but over time they usually lose.

How to avoid the pitfalls

  1. Prepare a business plan

    2. For new entrepreneur, a well-written business plan is essential to prepare the business success. Whithout a well-considered plan the business just goes along without any direction or course of action.

  2. Khow your business

    3. You need the right type of experience in business. Personal contact with suppliers and customers is one of the best way to gain that knowledge. Even contact with competitors is useful.

  3. Have a basic knowledge of finance

    4. An entrepreneur must depend on financial records and financial statements to know if the business is succeeding or failing. To understand what truly goin on, you must have a basic knowledge of accounting and finace. Finacial statements are reliable indicators of a small business health and can be quite helpful in showing you where there are problem areas in the business.

  4. Learn to manage people effectively

    5. No matter what kind of business you decide on, you must learn how to manage people. Every business depends on reliable, well-trained and motivated employees. Getting and keeping quality employees is a challenge for every employer.

  5. Entrepreneurial transition

    6. When a business start to grow the entrepreneur neeeds to give some power and control to others in the company. Some entrepreneurs finf it difficult to do this and still try to do everything themselves.
